üåüDestination Overview

Kandy, the cultural capital of Sri Lanka, is nestled amidst lush hills and tea plantations. It's renowned for the Temple of the Tooth Relic, a UNESCO World Heritage site and a deeply sacred Buddhist shrine. Kandy offers a captivating blend of history, spirituality, and natural beauty. Explore its vibrant markets, serene lake, and traditional dance performances. The city's rich heritage and stunning landscapes make it a must-visit destination for anyone seeking an authentic Sri Lankan experience. Kandy is a place where ancient traditions meet modern life, creating a truly unforgettable journey.

üóìÔ∏èBest Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kandy is from December to April, offering dry weather and comfortable temperatures for exploring the city.

üíéHidden Gems

Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue

This giant Buddha statue offers panoramic views of Kandy city and is less crowded than the Temple of the Tooth. A peaceful spot for reflection.

Udawatta Kele Sanctuary

A lush forest reserve offering hiking trails, diverse birdlife, and a tranquil escape from the city's hustle and bustle. Perfect for nature lovers.

Wales Park (Royal Palace Park)

Located above Kandy Lake, this park offers stunning views and a relaxing atmosphere. A great spot for a leisurely stroll or picnic.

Embekke Devalaya

An ancient temple known for its intricate wood carvings, showcasing scenes from daily life and mythology. A unique architectural gem.

Lankatilaka Temple

A stunning white temple perched atop a rock outcrop, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding countryside. A serene and spiritual experience.

üé≠Local Experiences

Attend a Kandyan Dance Performance

Witness the vibrant and energetic Kandyan dance, showcasing traditional costumes, drumming, and acrobatic feats. A cultural spectacle not to be missed.

Participate in a Cooking Class

Learn to prepare authentic Sri Lankan dishes using local ingredients and spices. A hands-on experience that will tantalize your taste buds.

Visit a Tea Plantation and Factory

Explore the rolling hills of a tea plantation, learn about the tea-making process, and enjoy a refreshing cup of Ceylon tea.

Offer Alms to Buddhist Monks

Wake up early and participate in the tradition of offering alms (food) to Buddhist monks. A humbling and spiritual experience.

Explore the Kandy Market

Immerse yourself in the bustling atmosphere of the Kandy Market, browsing through colorful stalls selling spices, fruits, vegetables, and local handicrafts.

üçΩÔ∏èFood & Dining

Rice and Curry

A staple Sri Lankan dish consisting of rice served with various vegetable and meat curries. A flavorful and satisfying meal.

Kottu Roti

Chopped roti bread stir-fried with vegetables, meat, and spices. A popular street food dish, customizable to your spice preference.

Hoppers (Appa)

Bowl-shaped pancakes made from rice flour and coconut milk. Enjoy them plain or with an egg in the center. Delicious for breakfast or dinner.

Wood Apple Juice

A unique and tangy juice made from the wood apple fruit. An acquired taste, but a refreshing and local experience.

Roti Shops

Small, local eateries serving a variety of roti, both savory and sweet. A budget-friendly and authentic dining experience.
